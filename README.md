# LogitSwap Judgemark (Creative Writing)
*An Adaptation of EQ-Bench's Judgemark v4*

This report presents the comparative evaluation framework and results for benchmarking candidate models to serve as an "LLM-as-a-Judge" using the **LogitSwap Judgemark (Creative Writing)** methodology. The goal of this framework is to assess and rank different models based on their evaluation quality, consistency, and resilience to biases.

---

## Evaluation Methodology

To evaluate candidate models under identical conditions, all judges are evaluated using a standardized continuous probability extraction and spatial symmetry protocol.

### 1. Logits Extraction & Soft Preference Probability
Rather than relying on discrete text generations (which are prone to formatting errors, formatting noise, and lack of nuance), candidate judges are evaluated using token log probabilities (logits) for the options `A` and `B`.

The raw preference probability is calculated using a softmax normalization over the logprobs of tokens `A` and `B`:

$$P(A) = \frac{e^{\text{logprob}_A}}{e^{\text{logprob}_A} + e^{\text{logprob}_B}} \times 100$$

$$P(B) = \frac{e^{\text{logprob}_B}}{e^{\text{logprob}_A} + e^{\text{logprob}_B}} \times 100$$

This continuous probability acts as a soft preference score, providing high-resolution data on the candidate's confidence level.

### 2. Position Swapping (Symmetry Check)
To mitigate positional/spatial bias (the tendency of LLMs to favor options placed in position `A` or `B`), every pairwise evaluation is performed twice:
1. **First Pass**: Response 1 is presented as `A`, Response 2 as `B`. Probability scores $S_1(A)$ and $S_1(B)$ are recorded.
2. **Second Pass**: Response 2 is presented as `A`, Response 1 as `B`. Probability scores $S_2(A)$ and $S_2(B)$ are recorded.
3. **Symmetric Integration**: The final expected probability score for Response 1 is the average of its score across both presentations:

$$\text{Final } P(\text{Response 1}) = \frac{S_1(A) + S_2(B)}{2}$$

## Benchmark Meta-Evaluation Metrics

To rank which candidate model is best qualified as a Judge LLM, two main tournament configurations (**Option A** and **Option B**) are used. These options measure the judge's discriminative resolution and stability.

### Option A: Prompt-Isolated Elo Separability
This metric measures how cleanly a candidate judge separates stronger writing from weaker writing at the individual prompt level.

* **Methodology**: For each prompt in the evaluation dataset, the candidate judge runs isolated Swiss tournaments (e.g., 5 rounds) across all writer models. This yields a prompt-specific Elo rating distribution for each writer model.
* **Separability Score**: We calculate **Omega-Squared ($\omega^2$)** (ANOVA effect size) and **Mean Absolute Cliff's Delta** across these prompt-level distributions.
  * **Omega-Squared ($\omega^2$)**: Represents the proportion of variance in prompt-specific Elo ratings explained solely by writer model identity. A higher value indicates the judge is highly resilient to prompt-specific noise.
  * **Cliff's Delta**: Quantifies the magnitude of difference between model distributions.
  * **Combined Separability**: Formulated to scale the separation capability on a 100-point index (omitting EQ-Bench's 0.75 normalization divisor):
    $$\text{Separability Score} = (0.5 \times \omega^2 + 0.5 \times d_{\text{cliff}}) \times 100$$

### Option B: Bootstrapped Tournament Stability
This metric measures the global consistency and reproducibility of the leaderboards generated by the candidate judge.

* **Methodology**: The prompt pool is resampled with replacement (bootstrapped) across multiple independent global tournament runs (e.g., 10 bootstraps of 20 rounds each).
* **Stability Score**: We measure the variance in global Elo ratings across bootstrap iterations. A high-performing judge will show low standard deviations ($\pm$ Elo error margin), indicating that its model rankings remain stable regardless of prompt variations.
